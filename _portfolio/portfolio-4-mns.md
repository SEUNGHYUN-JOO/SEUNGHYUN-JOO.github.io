## üîç Project Overview: Integrated M&S Development
This project aims to secure an integrated Modeling & Simulation (M&S) environment required for the development of **next-generation high-speed, long-range rotorcraft**. The focus was on building a high-performance computational framework and specialized analysis modules for unconventional rotorcraft configurations.

## üõ†Ô∏è Key Technical Contributions

### 1. High-Performance Python-C++ Interface & Wrapper
* **Motivation:** Overcoming the computational overhead of pure Python while maintaining its high versatility and intuitive structure.
* **Implementation:** Developed a **C++ based aerodynamic analysis core** and integrated it with Python via a custom **Interface and Wrapper**.
* **Efficiency:** Achieved high-speed computational performance while preserving an **object-oriented modeling environment**, enabling rapid design iterations.

### 2. Coaxial Rotor Aerodynamic Module (RISPECT III)
* **Module Development:** Developed and validated a **Coaxial Blade Element Theory (BET)** aerodynamic module within the **RISPECT III** module in AMADEVS framework.
* **Validation:** Verified the analysis accuracy by comparing results with **XH-59A flight test data** and other established codes (e.g., RCAS).

### 3. Sizing & Multidisciplinary Optimization
* **Configuration Comparison:** Conducted sizing and optimization for candidate platforms, specifically comparing **Tilt-Rotor** and **Coaxial-Pusher** configurations.
* **Mission-based Analysis:** Performed performance evaluations under various operational scenarios, including **High-speed Infiltration (Scenario A)** and **Medical Evacuation (Scenario B)**.
* **Weight & Power Analysis:** Derived trade-offs between required power, fuel consumption, and group weight (structure, propulsion, etc.) for each configuration.
